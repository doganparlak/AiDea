AR (AutoRegressive) Model

The AR model predicts future values based on a linear function of previous values in the time series. It assumes that the current value of the series can be explained by a linear combination of its past values. This model is useful for time series data that exhibit autocorrelation.

ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) Model

The ARIMA model combines the AR model with differencing (to make the time series stationary) and a moving average component. It is used for time series data that show trends and seasonality. ARIMA models are suitable for series where the past values have a linear relationship with future values and the series exhibits non-stationarity.

SARIMA (Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) Model

The SARIMA model extends ARIMA by incorporating seasonal effects into the forecasting process. It includes seasonal differencing and seasonal autoregressive and moving average components. SARIMA is ideal for time series data that exhibit clear seasonal patterns.

SARIMAX (Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average with Exogenous Regressors) Model

The SARIMAX model builds upon SARIMA by including external factors or exogenous variables that might affect the time series. It can handle complex seasonal patterns and external influences, making it useful for forecasting when additional relevant variables are known.

VAR (Vector AutoRegression) Model

The VAR model is used for multivariate time series forecasting where the interdependencies among multiple time series are important. It forecasts each variable as a linear function of past values of itself and past values of all other variables in the system.

VARMA (Vector AutoRegressive Moving Average) Model

The VARMA model extends VAR by including moving average components. It captures both the autoregressive and moving average structures in multivariate time series data, improving the forecasting accuracy for systems with complex relationships.

GARCH (Generalized AutoRegressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity) Model

The GARCH model is designed for time series with changing volatility over time, such as financial data. It models the volatility (variance) of the series and is useful for forecasting future volatility based on past data, making it valuable in risk management and financial forecasting.
